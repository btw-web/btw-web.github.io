Bikepacking with Kids: Tips for a Family Adventure

Bikepacking with kids can be an incredibly rewarding family adventure. It can help your children develop a love for the outdoors, teach them self-reliance, and provide opportunities for bonding and creating lasting memories.

However, bikepacking with children also requires careful planning, safety considerations, andthe right gear. In this article, we share tips and advice to make your bikepacking trip with kids a success.

Essential Gear

  1. Bike: Choose a bike that is suitable for your child’s age, height, and skill levels. Make sure it is comfortable, properly fitted, and able to handle your bikepacking gear.

  2. Bikepacking bags: Use specially designed bikepacking bags to carry your gear, food, water, and clothes. These bags are lightweight, waterproof, and easy to attach to your bike.

  3. Tent: Invest in a lightweight tent that can accommodate your family comfortably. Ensure that it is easy to set up and take down, and provides adequate protection against wind, rain, and insects.

  4. Sleeping bags and pads: Choose lightweight and compact sleeping bags and pads that provide warmth and comfort. Opt for those that pack small and are easy to carry on a bike.

  5. Cooking equipment: Bring a portable stove, fuel, and cookware to prepare meals and hot drinks. Make sure that you pack food that is easy to prepare, nutritious, and appealing to your children.

  6. First-aid kit: Carry a comprehensive first-aid kit that includes essentials such as bandages, antiseptic, ibuprofen, and insect repellent.

Route Planning

When planning your bikepacking route with kids, consider the following factors:

  1. Distance and terrain: Choose a distance and terrain that is suitable for your children’s age, fitness, and experience levels. Avoid overly challenging or steep routes that may tire them.

  2. Trail conditions: Research the trail conditions beforehand to avoid road blocks, closures, or dangerous sections. Check the weather forecast and plan for any possible changes in weather.

  3. Campsite locations: Identify suitable campsites along the route that offer adequate facilities, such as water sources, toilets, and fire pits.

  4. Emergency contacts: Make a list of emergency contacts and share it with everyone in your group. Ensure that you have access to a map, compass, and GPS in case of emergencies.

Safety Tips

Bikepacking with kids requires careful consideration of safety issues. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  1. Helmet: Ensure that everyone wears a properly fitting helmet at all times when biking.

  2. Visibility: Use reflective gear, lights, and bright clothing to enhance visibility on the road.

  3. Sun protection: Pack sunscreen, hats, and sunglasses to protect against the sun’s harmful rays.

  4. Hydration: Ensure that everyone drinks enough water to stay hydrated, especially in hot weather.

  5. Bug spray: Use insect repellent to protect against ticks, mosquitoes, and other insects.

  6. Navigation: Teach your children how to read maps, use a compass, and follow trail markers to avoid getting lost.

Conclusion

Bikepacking with kids can be a fun and rewarding family experience if planned and executed properly. Remember to choose the right gear, plan an appropriate route, and prioritize safety considerations. By following these tips, you can embark on a bikepacking adventure with your children that will create lifelong memories.
