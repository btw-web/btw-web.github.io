Bikepacking is undoubtedly one of the most thrilling ways to travel, as it allows you to venture into remote areas, experience the joy of being under the open sky, and get closer to nature. It's also a great way to stay active, get some exercise, and explore different terrains. However, to make the most of a bikepacking trip, you must have a solid plan and navigation tools at your disposal. Fortunately, there are plenty of apps that can make the trip more enjoyable. To help plan and navigate your next bikepacking adventure, consider these best apps:

Komoot

Komoot is a popular app for outdoor enthusiasts that includes comprehensive route planning, weather forecasting, and even offline maps. With Komoot, you can easily plan your bikepacking routes, edit them on the go, and even share them with friends. The app also shows you detailed elevation profiles and real-time traffic conditions, making it easier to choose the best bikepacking route possible. Komoot is available on both Android and iOS platforms, and you can choose to use its free or premium version.

Trailforks

Trailforks is a must-have app for mountain bikers, offering a comprehensive guide to more than 220,000 miles of off-road trails all over the world. With Trailforks, you can explore new trails, discover trails near you, and even record the trails you've ridden. The app also provides real-time weather forecasts, trail alerts, and a detailed trail rating system. In short, Trailforks is a comprehensive tool that can help you plan, navigate, and enjoy your bikepacking trip to the fullest. Trailforks is available on both Android and iOS platforms, and you can use it for free.

Gaia GPS

Gaia GPS is another popular app for outdoor enthusiasts, featuring offline maps, hiking and biking trails, and even topographic and satellite maps. With Gaia GPS, you can plan your bikepacking routes, navigate them offline, and even share them with your friends. The app also supports other outdoor activities, such as hunting, fishing, and camping. Gaia GPS is available on both Android and iOS platforms, and you can choose to use its free or paid version.

Bike Computer

Bike Computer is a simple yet effective app that can turn your smartphone into a powerful bike computer. With Bike Computer, you can track your speed, distance, and time, as well as record your route and share it with others. The app also includes voice feedback, so you can keep your eyes on the road while still being aware of your performance. Bike Computer is available on both Android and iOS platforms, and you can use it for free.

Google Maps

Of course, no list of best apps for planning and navigating a bikepacking trip would be complete without mentioning Google Maps. The app offers comprehensive maps, driving directions, and even real-time traffic updates, making it easier to avoid traffic jams and find the quickest route. While Google Maps is not specifically designed for bikepacking, it still serves as a powerful navigation tool, allowing you to plan your trip, track your progress, and find your way back in case you get lost. Google Maps is available on both Android and iOS platforms, and you can use it for free.

In conclusion, bikepacking can be an immensely rewarding experience, especially if you have the right apps to help you plan and navigate. Whether you prefer detailed route planning, comprehensive trail guides, or a simple bike computer, there's an app out there that can make your bikepacking adventure even more enjoyable. So why not download a few of these apps and start planning your next bikepacking trip today?
