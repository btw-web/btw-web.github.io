The Best Bikepacking Trails in South America and Africa

If you're looking for a thrilling adventure, bikepacking is an incredible way to explore the great outdoors! South America and Africa are home to some of the most beautiful and challenging bikepacking trails on the planet. From remote mountain passes to winding desert roads, the opportunities for unforgettable experiences are endless. Here are some of the best bikepacking trails in South America and Africa that should be on every adventurer's list.

South America

1. Carretera Austral, Chile

Stretching over 1,200 km through Chile's wild and rugged Patagonia landscape, the Carretera Austral is one of South America's most iconic bikepacking routes. With stunning glacier-fed lakes, snow-capped mountains and dense rainforests, the scenery is simply breathtaking. The terrain ranges from challenging gravel roads to steep mountain passes, making this trail a true test of endurance.

2. Salar de Uyuni, Bolivia

Bolivia's Salar de Uyuni is the largest salt flat in the world, covering over 10,000 square kilometers. The vast, otherworldly landscape is something straight out of a science fiction movie, making it a must-visit destination for adventure seekers. The area is also home to several geothermal hot springs and volcanoes, creating a unique and surreal environment.

3. Inca Divide Route, Peru

The Inca Divide Route is a challenging and rewarding trail that weaves its way through the Peruvian Andes, following ancient Inca footpaths and mule tracks. Along the way, you'll encounter stunning views of snow-capped peaks, lush valleys and traditional Andean communities. The terrain is tough, with steep climbs and descents, but the sense of accomplishment at the end is worth it.

Africa

1. Atlas Traverse, Morocco

The Atlas Traverse is an epic bikepacking route that takes you through the rugged Atlas Mountains of Morocco, from the Atlantic coast to the Sahara Desert. The trail passes through traditional Berber villages and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding peaks and gorges. The terrain is challenging, with steep climbs and rocky descents, but the cultural experiences and stunning scenery make it well worth the effort.

2. Cape Epic, South Africa

The Cape Epic is one of the most popular mountain bike races in the world, but you don't have to be a pro athlete to experience this incredible trail. Spanning over 700 km through the Western Cape Province of South Africa, the route takes you through pristine wilderness areas and rugged terrain. Along the way, you'll encounter wildlife such as baboons, antelope and ostriches, adding to the thrill of the adventure.

3. The Rift Valley, Kenya

Kenya's Rift Valley is a breathtakingly beautiful landscape that is ideal for bikepacking enthusiasts. From the slopes of Mount Kenya to the shores of Lake Victoria, the trail spans over 800 km of varied terrain, including rainforests, savannahs and volcanic plateaus. The area is also home to traditional Maasai communities, offering a glimpse into life in rural Kenya.

Whether you're an experienced bikepacker or a beginner, these trails offer incredible adventures that will leave you with memories to last a lifetime. So pack your bags, grab your bike and get ready for the ride of your life!
