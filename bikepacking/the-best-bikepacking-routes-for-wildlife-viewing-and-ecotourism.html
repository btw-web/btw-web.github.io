The Best Bikepacking Routes for Wildlife Viewing and Ecotourism

Bikepacking is a fantastic way to explore the great outdoors and immerse yourself in nature. If you are an eco-conscious adventurer and love wildlife, choosing bikepacking routes that allow you to encounter fascinating creatures is a great option.

Here are some of the best bikepacking routes for wildlife viewing and ecotourism adventures that you must try:

Great Divide Mountain Bike Route

The Great Divide Mountain Bike Route is a 2,700-mile-long trail that runs from Banff, Alberta, Canada, to Antelope Wells, New Mexico, USA. The trail takes you through breathtaking scenery, including the vast wilderness of the Rocky Mountains. It's also home to a diverse range of wildlife, such as grizzly bears, wolves, elk, moose, and bighorn sheep.

Trans Ecuador Mountain Bike Route

The Trans Ecuador Mountain Bike Route is a 500-mile-long trail that runs from Quito to Vilcabamba, Ecuador. The trail takes you through the stunning Andes Mountains and a range of climatic zones. You will encounter a range of fascinating creatures, such as Andean bears, condors, llamas, and vicu√±as.

Munda Biddi Trail

The Munda Biddi Trail is a 1,000-kilometer-long trail that runs through Western Australia's natural bushland and forests. The trail runs from Mundaring, near Perth, to Albany, along the south coast of Western Australia. The trail is home to an incredible range of wildlife, such as kangaroos, echidnas, wallabies, and a diverse range of birdlife.

The Dempster Highway

The Dempster Highway is a 740-kilometer-long dirt road that runs from Dawson, Yukon Territory to Inuvik, Northwest Territories, Canada. The road takes you through the heart of the Canadian wilderness and offers amazing wildlife viewing opportunities. You can see herds of caribou, grizzly bears, and other unique animals in their natural habitat.

The Wild Atlantic Way

The Wild Atlantic Way is a 2,500-kilometer-long route that runs along Ireland's west coast. The route offers breathtaking ocean views, rugged coastline, and diverse ecosystems. Along the way, you will encounter a range of wildlife such as seals, dolphins, whales, puffins, and a variety of seabirds.

Bikepacking is an excellent way to explore natural landscapes and experience meaningful, sustainable tourism. If you love encountering fascinating wildlife, choosing a bikepacking route that offers the chance to see wild animals up close is an experience you won't forget. So pack your bags, hop on your bike and explore the beauty of the wilderness!
