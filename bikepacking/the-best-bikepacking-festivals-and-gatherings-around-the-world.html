The Best Bikepacking Festivals and Gatherings Around the World

Bikepacking is a thrilling way to explore the beauty of nature on two wheels while camping under the stars. And it's even more enjoyable when you're part of a community of like-minded people. For that reason, bikepacking gatherings and festivals have become increasingly popular over the years. Here are some of the best bikepacking festivals and gatherings around the world that cater to all types of riders and interests.

1. Grinduro

Grinduro is a bike race that takes place in California, Scotland, and Canada. Riders cover 60-100 miles in a single day, with timed segments that test riders' endurance and technical skills. Grinduro also includes live music, camping, and great food, making it an ideal event for adventurous souls.

2. The Transcontinental

The Transcontinental is a self-supported race that spans from Belgium to Turkey, covering 4,000 km. The race is open to all types of bikes, and riders have to navigate through checkpoints while carrying everything they need, including food and water. The Transcontinental is not for the faint-hearted, but it's an exciting opportunity for those who love to push their limits.

3. Swift Campout

Swift Campout is a global event that celebrates the solstice and encourages bikepacking enthusiasts worldwide to go camping by bike. The event is perfect for beginners, as it's less of a race and more of a relaxed gathering. Riders are encouraged to explore their local surroundings and share their experiences with others online.

4. Tour Divide

The Tour Divide is a grueling race that spans from Banff, Canada to Antelope Wells, New Mexico, covering 2,745 miles of rugged terrain. Although Tour Divide is a race, many riders also participate for the personal challenge, and it's not uncommon for them to form bonds with fellow racers over the course of their journey.

5. The Trans-Pyrenees

The Trans-Pyrenees is an unforgettable adventure that takes you through the heart of the Pyrenees between France and Spain. The route covers 800 km with 98% of off-road tracks, including some technical single tracks. The Trans-Pyrenees is not a race but a chance to explore one of Europe's most stunning mountain ranges.

Whether you're a seasoned bikepacker or a beginner, the above bikepacking festivals and gatherings offer something for everyone. They're not only great opportunities to meet fellow riders and explore new trails, but they also provide a chance to challenge yourself, push your limits, and make unforgettable memories. Happy bikepacking!
