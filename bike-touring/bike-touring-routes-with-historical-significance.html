Bike Touring Routes with Historical Significance

Are you an adventurous cyclist searching for a unique way to combine exercise and history? Here are some bike touring routes that take you through scenic routes rich in historical significance.

Route 66 in the United States

For a truly classic American experience, follow the path of the historic Route 66. This iconic stretch of highway, opened in 1926, stretches from Chicago to Santa Monica, California. Along the way, you'll pedal through small towns that embody the spirit of America's love affair with the open road. Don't forget to stop at memorable landmarks like the Cadillac Ranch or the Wigwam Motel. The backdrop of the Western landscapes adds to the appeal of this scenic route.

The Iron Curtain Trail in Europe

The Iron Curtain Trail (ICT) stretches over 6,675 km, covering European countries from the Arctic Circle to the Black Sea. This trail, which follows the former Iron Curtain that separated Europe during the Cold War, offers an incredible opportunity to witness the transformation of Europe over the last century. From charming cities like Prague to remote villages, the ICT takes you through diverse landscapes that reflect the intense history of Europe during the war.

The Great Wall of China Cycle

Cycle along the Great Wall of China to experience this iconic structure up close. The journey is truly awe-inspiring as you gear up to embark on a journey along the 8,850 kilometers long trail that spans across the hills, valleys, and mountains of China. You will be rewarded with the breathtaking views of the wall and the delightfully scenic rural areas. The intense history of the wall and the surrounding areas that reflect China's ancient culture is a bonus!

The Camino de Santiago in Spain

The Camino de Santiago's history dates back to the 9th century when Saint James' remains were found in Santiago de Compostela. The pilgrimage from various regions of Spain remained popular for centuries. Today, thousands of people from all over the world participate in this pilgrimage to Santiago de Compostela. The trail takes you through stunning vistas, medieval churches, ancient museums, and some of the most scenic terrain of Northern Spain.

Cycling is a great way to immerse yourself in different cultures and experience new ways of life. Combine your love for cycling with history and witness some of the world's most iconic locations. These bike touring routes with historical significance offer an education in world affairs and an appreciation of the world's wonderful diversity.
