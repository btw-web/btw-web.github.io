Finding Safe and Scenic Routes for Bike Touring

Bike touring is an excellent way to explore a new environment, get exercise, and enjoy the beauty of nature. However, finding a safe and scenic route can be challenging, especially for those who are new to the area. Here are some tips for discovering safe and beautiful bike touring routes that offer stunning scenery and minimal safety risks.

1. Research the area

Before setting out on a bike tour, research the area you plan to explore. You can start by reviewing online maps, reading guidebooks, or consulting with local bike clubs. By doing so, you'll be able to identify potential routes that will offer you the scenery you crave without exposing you to major safety risks.

2. Check the difficulty level

When choosing a route, make sure to check the route's difficulty level. Choose a route that is not too difficult for your skill level to avoid unnecessary strain or risk. By choosing a route that is comfortable for you, you'll be able to enjoy the scenery and the ride itself.

3. Avoid high-traffic areas

When looking for a safe and scenic route for bike touring, it's important to avoid high-traffic areas. Instead, choose roads and paths that are less busy to minimize the risk of accidents or collisions. Today, many cities have dedicated bike lanes or bike paths that offer safe and picturesque routes for travelers.

4. Focus on the scenery

The scenery is a critical factor for a pleasant and rewarding bike touring experience. Therefore, focus on routes that offer stunning views of the local landscape. Coastal or mountain paths, parks or nature reserves are often fantastic options. However, make sure to check for potential hazards, such as sharp cliffs or unstable terrains before setting out on your adventure.

5. Be prepared for the unexpected

Despite your best efforts to plan a safe and scenic route, it's always best to be prepared for the unexpected. Make sure to carry enough water, snacks, and first-aid supplies. Ensure that you have a map or GPS and a charged phone to navigate safely. Above all, inform friends or family of your route and expected time of return in case of emergencies.

Bottom Line

With the tips above, you can easily find safe and scenic routes for your next biking tour. Remember to research the area thoroughly, choose routes based on your skill level and the scenery and avoid high-traffic areas.
