Bike touring in remote areas: Tips for safety and self-reliance

Bike touring is one of the best ways to explore the great outdoors while getting a good workout in. But when you’re venturing into remote areas, safety and self-reliance are key to ensure a fun and successful trip. Here are some tips to stay safe and self-sufficient during your bike tour:

1. Plan ahead

Before embarking on any bike tour, take the time to plan your route, pack properly, and consider the weather conditions. It’s also a good idea to let someone know your itinerary and expected return date.

2. Carry emergency supplies

Whether you’re going on a short or long ride, always bring emergency supplies such as a first aid kit, water, food, and a navigation system. It’s also helpful to have extra bike parts such as a spare tire, pump, and tube in case of a mechanical issue.

3. Stay aware of your surroundings

When bike touring in remote areas, it’s important to pay attention to your surroundings, especially when it comes to wildlife. Stay alert and avoid dangerous areas such as steep drops or busy roads.

4. Bring a communication device

In case of emergency, it’s important to have a way to communicate with others. Bring a cell phone or a satellite communication device to keep in touch with loved ones, emergency services, or other members of your touring group.

5. Familiarize yourself with basic bike repair skills

Bike mechanical issues are inevitable during long-distance rides. Make sure you’re familiar with basic bike repair skills such as fixing a flat tire, adjusting brakes and gears, and maintaining your bike chain.

6. Follow the principles of Leave No Trace

Always practice the principles of Leave No Trace when bike touring in remote areas. These principles include disposing of waste properly, leaving natural features as you found them, and respecting wildlife.

Bike touring in remote areas can be an exhilarating and rewarding experience. By following these tips, you can ensure a safe and self-reliant adventure.
