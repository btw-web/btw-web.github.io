Bikepacking Electronics: Charging, Lighting, and Communication Devices

Bikepacking can be an exhilarating activity for adventurers who love traveling on two wheels. With the right bikepacking gear, you can take on long distances, rugged terrain, and all kinds of weather conditions. However, one aspect of bikepacking that often receives less attention than it deserves is electronics.

In this article, we will cover the essential gear you need for bikepacking, including devices for charging, lighting, and communication.

Charging Devices

When you're out bikepacking, you'll need some way to charge your electronics. Fortunately, there are portable charging devices that are perfect for this purpose. Two of the most popular options are power banks and solar chargers.

Power banks are compact batteries that can be charged before your trip and then used to charge your phone, GPS, or any other USB device on the go. They usually come with multiple USB ports so you can charge several devices at once.

Solar chargers, on the other hand, use the sun's energy to directly charge your devices. They are a great option for bikepackers who will be spending a lot of time outside in the sun. Some of the best solar chargers are portable and foldable, making them easy to pack.

Lighting Devices

If you are planning on doing any bikepacking at night or in low-light conditions, you'll need some lighting devices to keep you safe and help you see where you're going. The following are some options to consider:

  • Headlamps: These are great for when you need to see in the dark but still need your hands free to steer your bike.
  • Bike lights: These can be mounted on your handlebars, seat post, or helmet. They are especially important if you will be riding on roads with cars.
  • Lanterns: These are great for setting up camp or lighting up your tent.

Communication Devices

Finally, it's important to consider communication devices for emergencies or keeping in touch with others. The following are some options to consider:

  • Two-way radios: These are great for keeping in touch with your traveling companions, especially if you'll be separated for long periods of time.
  • Personal locator beacons (PLBs): These are essential for emergencies when you need to call for help. They are GPS-enabled and can transmit your location to rescue teams.
  • Satellite Messengers: These devices use satellite technology to send messages and location information so you can communicate even in remote areas without cell phone coverage.

In conclusion, bikepacking electronics are an important aspect of your gear. By bringing along the right charging devices, lighting devices, and communication devices, you will have the tools you need to stay safe, connected, and ready for anything your bikepacking adventure throws your way.
