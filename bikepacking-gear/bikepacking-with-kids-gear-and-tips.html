Bikepacking with Kids: Gear and Tips

Bikepacking with kids can be a fun and rewarding experience for the whole family. It's a great way to spend quality time together, explore the outdoors, and challenge yourselves physically. However, it requires some planning and preparation to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip. In this article, we'll cover some of the essential gear and tips for a successful bikepacking trip with kids.

Essential Gear

1. Bikes and Trailers

It’s essential to have the right gear for bikepacking with kids. You’ll need bikes for everyone and, if your children are too young to bike on their own, a trailer to pull them along. It's essential to make sure that the bikes and trailers are in good condition before you start your trip.

2. Sleeping Gear

A good night's sleep is crucial to enjoying your bikepacking trip, especially for the kids. Make sure you bring a comfortable sleeping bag, sleeping pad, and a tent that’s big enough for everyone. If there are no campsites, you can find a suitable place to camp out in nature.

3. Clothing

When it comes to clothing, you should have comfortable and appropriate gear for the weather you'll encounter on your trip. Dress in layers and bring rain gear in case of a sudden downpour. Remember to pack enough changes of clothing for everyone.

4. Food and Water

Bring enough food and snacks for everyone on your trip, including a good breakfast and a hot meal for dinner. You can pack dry foods like cereal, oatmeal, and energy bars. Don't forget to bring plenty of water and a portable water filter or purification tablets.

Tips for a Successful Trip

1. Plan Your Trip

Before you start your trip, research your route and map out where you'll camp each night. Make sure you plan for breaks throughout the day and take into account the level of physical activity that’s required for everyone. Interactive maps can help you find bike-friendly roads and trails.

2. Safety First

Safety should be your number one priority when you're bikepacking with kids. Make sure you have first aid kits, bike lights, and reflectors. Teach your children safety rules, such as traffic safety and proper helmet wear, so they are aware of how to behave on the road.

3. Keep the Kids Engaged

To keep kids excited and engaged during the trip, you'll need to plan fun activities. Bring along things like a frisbee, a deck of cards, or coloring books. Look out for interesting natural landmarks on your route and take breaks to learn about them. Involve the kids in setting up camp and cooking meals.

4. Don't Overdo It

Make sure you don't push your children too hard. Keep the pace slow and steady, and take breaks when necessary. Adjust your route to your stamina and comfort levels. Remember, the goal is to have fun and enjoy nature together, not to break records for distance or speed.

Conclusion

Bikepacking with kids can be a great way to spend quality time with your family and explore the outdoors. By following these tips and having the right gear, you can ensure a successful trip that's both safe and enjoyable for everyone. Remember to plan your trip, prioritize safety, keep the kids engaged, and don't overdo it. Enjoy the adventure!
