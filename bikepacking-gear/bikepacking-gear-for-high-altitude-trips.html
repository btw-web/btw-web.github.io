Bikepacking Gear for High-Altitude Trips

Bikepacking is a thrilling way to explore the great outdoors, but when it comes to high-altitude trips, there are a few extra considerations to take into account. In addition to standard bikepacking gear, you'll need to take into consideration the thin air, changing weather, and vastly different terrain. With that in mind, here are some essential pieces of gear to ensure a successful and safe high-altitude bikepacking trip.

Lightweight and Durable Tent

A lightweight and durable tent is essential for bikepacking, but it's especially important for high-altitude trips where weight and space are at a premium. Look for tents that are specifically designed for high-altitudes, with features like steeper walls and better ventilation. A great option is the Big Agnes Copper Spur HV UL.

High-quality Sleeping Bag

A high-quality sleeping bag that can withstand the harsh conditions of high-altitude environments is a must. Look for a bag rated to at least 20 degrees Fahrenheit and made from high-quality materials. The NEMO Disco 15 is an excellent choice, as it is specifically designed for camping at high-altitude.

Insulated Sleeping Pad

An insulated sleeping pad is a must for staying warm and comfortable during high-altitude nights. Look for pads that are lightweight, durable, and provide ample insulation. The Therm-a-Rest NeoAir XLite is an excellent choice, as it is both lightweight and warm.

Waterproof and Windproof Jacket

The weather at high-altitude can change rapidly, making a waterproof and windproof jacket essential for any bikepacking trip. Look for jackets made from high-quality materials that will withstand harsh weather conditions. The Patagonia Houdini is a great choice, as it is lightweight, waterproof, and windproof.

Wide-range Gearing

When it comes to high-altitude bikepacking, having a wide-range gearing system is crucial. Look for bikes with at least a 1:1 gear ratio and choose gearing that is suitable for steep climbs and long descents. The Salsa Fargo is an excellent bike for high-altitude bikepacking, with a range of gears designed for tackling mountainous terrain.

Hydration System

Staying hydrated is critical at high-altitudes, where the air is thin and dehydration can occur more easily. Look for a hydration system that is easy to use, lightweight, and can hold enough water for a minimum of two hours of riding. The CamelBak M.U.L.E. is an excellent option, as it is lightweight and can hold up to three liters of water.

Navigation System

Navigating high-altitude terrain can be challenging, so having a reliable navigation system is essential. Look for GPS devices or smartphone apps that can offer you a clear route and help you navigate tricky terrain. The Garmin Edge 530 is an excellent choice, with advanced navigation features and long battery life.

Final Thoughts

Bikepacking in high-altitude environments can be a thrilling and rewarding experience, but it requires careful planning and preparation. Choosing the right gear will ensure that you stay safe, comfortable, and enjoy your trip to the fullest. With the gear listed above, you'll be well-prepared for your next high-altitude bikepacking adventure.
