Bikepacking Gear for Photography and Videography

Bikepacking is a great way to explore the outdoors and go on an adventure, especially if you love photography and videography. Being out in the wilderness with nothing but your bike, a camera, and some essential equipment can be extremely challenging. But with the right gear, it can also be an incredibly rewarding experience. In this article, we'll take a look at some of the essential gear you need for bikepacking photography and videography.

Camera

The first essential piece of gear for bikepacking photography and videography is a camera. There are a multitude of cameras available, from point-and-shoots to professional-grade DSLRs. Choose a camera that fits your budget, your skill level, and your style of photography. If you're going to be biking, you may want to consider a smaller camera that's lightweight and easy to carry. Mirrorless cameras are an excellent choice for bikepacking, as they're smaller and lighter than DSLRs and still offer excellent picture quality. Another popular option is a GoPro camera, as they're durable and compact, making them perfect for outdoor adventures.

Tripod

A tripod is an essential piece of equipment for any photographer or videographer. A lightweight and sturdy tripod is even more crucial for bikepacking, as you want to be able to set up your shots quickly and easily. A GorillaPod is an excellent option for bikepacking, as it's small and lightweight, and can be attached to almost anything. Alternatively, a compact, lightweight tripod like the Manfrotto BeFree Carbon Fiber Tripod is an excellent choice for bikepacking photography and videography.

Gear Storage

When you're on a bikepacking adventure, you need to be able to carry all your gear with you. A backpack or a set of panniers is an obvious choice, but there are some gear storage options that are better suited to photography and videography. One option is a camera-specific backpack like the Lowepro ProTactic BP 450 AW II. This backpack has customizable dividers and plenty of room for all your camera gear, as well as your bikepacking essentials. Another option is a handlebar bag, like the Ortlieb Ultimate Six.

Lighting

Lighting is crucial for photography and videography, and can make the difference between an ordinary shot and an extraordinary one. When you're bikepacking, you don't want to carry around bulky lighting equipment, so you need to be creative. One option is to take advantage of natural light, shooting during the golden hour, or capturing sunsets and sunrises. Another option is to use small, portable LED lights, like the Lume Cube, which can be easily attached to your camera or bike. They're also waterproof and durable, making them perfect for outdoor adventures.

Conclusion

Bikepacking is an excellent way to explore the outdoors and capture your adventures through photography and videography. With the right gear, you can take stunning photos and videos of your journey while enjoying the beautiful scenery around you. Choose a camera that suits your budget, a lightweight tripod for quick and easy setup, the right gear storage to easily carry everything you need, and flexible lighting options like natural light or portable LED lights. With this gear, you'll be ready to embark on your next bikepacking adventure in style.
