Bike Touring During a Pandemic: Equipment and Safety Considerations

Bike touring is an exciting and adventurous activity that allows you to explore new places, meet new people, and challenge yourself physically. However, with the ongoing pandemic, it's essential to take extra precautions to ensure your safety and the safety of those around you. Here are some equipment and safety considerations to keep in mind when planning your bike tour during the pandemic.

Essential Equipment

  1. Bike: Your bike is your primary piece of equipment for touring. Ensure that it's in good condition, with properly inflated tires, well-oiled chain, and working brakes. Check with your local bike shop if you need to have any repairs or maintenance done.

  2. Navigation tools: A GPS device or a map and compass are essential for navigating your route. It's important to avoid crowds and high-traffic areas, so plan your route carefully and know where you're going before heading out.

  3. Camping gear: If you plan to camp during your bike tour, you'll need to pack essential camping gear such as a tent, sleeping bag, stove, and fuel. Make sure all your gear is clean and in good condition.

  4. Face mask and hand sanitizer: These items are essential for protecting yourself and others from the virus. Pack enough masks and sanitizer for your entire trip, and use them as needed.

  5. First aid kit: A well-stocked first aid kit is always important, but even more so during a pandemic. Pack items such as bandages, antiseptic wipes, and over-the-counter pain medication.

Safety Considerations

  1. Social distancing: It's important to practice social distancing during your bike tour. Keep a safe distance of at least six feet from others, avoid crowded areas, and wear a mask when in close proximity to others.

  2. Stay informed: Stay up-to-date with the latest guidelines and regulations from local health authorities in the areas you plan to visit. Be aware of any changes or restrictions that might impact your tour.

  3. Plan ahead: Plan your route carefully, and be aware of any potential hazards or areas that might be closed due to the pandemic. Have a backup plan in case your route needs to be altered.

  4. Be self-sufficient: Avoid relying on others for resources such as food, water, or shelter. Bring enough supplies with you to be self-sufficient throughout your tour.

  5. Be flexible: Understand that your tour might not go exactly as planned due to the pandemic. Be flexible and prepared to adapt to any changes or challenges that may arise.

In conclusion, while the pandemic presents new challenges for bike touring, it's still possible to have a safe and enjoyable trip with the right equipment and safety measures. By following these recommendations, you can explore new places, meet new people, and challenge yourself while staying safe and healthy. Happy touring!
