Bike Touring Electronics and Charging Devices

When embarking on a bike touring trip, having the right electronics and charging devices can make all the difference. From staying connected with loved ones to using navigation apps and capturing beautiful photos, there are a variety of reasons why you may want to bring electronics on your trip. However, with limited space and access to power sources, it’s important to choose wisely. In this article, we’ll explore the best electronics and charging devices for bike touring trips.

Essential Electronics for Bike Touring

Smartphone

Your smartphone will likely be one of the most important electronics you bring on your trip. Not only can it serve as a GPS, but you can also use it to stay connected with loved ones, take photos, and even listen to music or audiobooks on long rides.

Camera

If you’re interested in photography, bringing a camera on your trip can be a fantastic way to capture memories and beautiful landscapes. Consider bringing a lightweight mirrorless or point-and-shoot camera, as these tend to be less bulky than DSLRs.

Headphones

If you plan on listening to music or podcasts during your trip, headphones are a must-have. Opt for lightweight, wireless headphones that won’t weigh you down or get tangled up in your gear.

Bike Computer or GPS

To track your progress and navigate your route, consider bringing a dedicated bike computer or GPS device. These can provide more accurate data than a smartphone, and you won’t have to worry about draining your phone battery.

Charging Devices for Bike Touring

Power Banks

Power banks are a must-have for any bike touring trip. These portable battery packs can charge your electronics on-the-go, and they come in a variety of capacities and sizes. Look for a power bank with at least 10,000mAh to ensure you’ll have enough power for multiple charges.

Dynamo Hubs

For longer trips where you won’t have access to power sources, consider installing a dynamo hub on your bike. These hubs generate power as you ride, so you can charge your devices while you pedal. Keep in mind that dynamo hubs can be expensive and may require professional installation.

Solar Panels

If you’re touring in an area with plenty of sunlight, a solar panel can be a great way to keep your devices charged. Look for a lightweight, foldable solar panel that can be easily attached to your bike or backpack.

Wall Charger

If you do have access to a power source, a wall charger can be a reliable and fast way to charge your devices. Look for a charger with multiple USB ports so you can charge multiple devices at once.

Conclusion

When it comes to bike touring electronics and charging devices, there are a variety of options to choose from. Make sure you consider your needs and priorities when selecting your devices and packing your gear. Remember to also pack any necessary cables, adapters, and mounts to ensure your electronics function properly on your trip. With the right tech gear in tow, you’ll be ready to capture amazing memories and stay connected with the world while enjoying the great outdoors.
