Best Bike Touring Shoes and Pedals

Bike touring is an amazing way to explore new places and challenge yourself physically, but it can also be tough on your feet and legs if you're not properly equipped. That's why it's important to invest in the right shoes and pedals for your needs. Here are some of the best options available:

Shoes

1. Giro Rumble VR Cycling Shoes

These shoes are perfect for bike touring thanks to their rugged design, comfortable fit, and versatile outsole. They have a breathable mesh upper that helps keep your feet cool and dry, and a Vibram rubber outsole that provides excellent traction on and off the bike. The laces make for a snug fit, and the shoes come in a variety of colors to suit your style.

2. Shimano SH-MT3 Cycling Shoes

If you're looking for a more budget-friendly option, the Shimano SH-MT3 is a solid choice. These shoes are lightweight and comfortable, with a breathable mesh upper and a rubber outsole that grips well on a variety of surfaces. They also have a recessed cleat design that makes walking easier when you're off the bike.

3. Pearl iZUMi X-Alp Journey Cycling Shoes

The Pearl iZUMi X-Alp Journey shoes are another great option for bike touring. They have a lightweight and breathable upper, a full-length nylon plate for added stability, and a lugged outsole that provides traction on any terrain. The shoes also have a two-bolt cleat design that works with most mountain bike pedals.

Pedals

1. Shimano PD-M520 Pedals

The Shimano PD-M520 pedals are a popular choice for bike touring thanks to their durability, value, and ease of use. They have a double-sided design that makes clipping in and out a breeze, and a wide platform that provides plenty of support for your feet. The pedals also have adjustable tension settings to customize the feel to your liking.

2. Crankbrothers Eggbeater 3 Pedals

For those who prefer a minimalist pedal design, the Crankbrothers Eggbeater 3 pedals are a great option. These pedals have a four-sided entry system that allows for easy clipping in and out from any angle, and a lightweight design that won't weigh you down on long rides. The pedals also have a solid construction that stands up to wear and tear.

3. Shimano PD-T8000 Pedals

If you're planning on doing some off-road riding on your bike tour, the Shimano PD-T8000 pedals are a good choice. These pedals have a wide platform that provides stability on rough terrain, as well as a recessed cleat design that makes walking easier. They also have adjustable tension settings and a low-maintenance design that's perfect for long-distance touring.

Conclusion

When it comes to bike touring, having the right shoes and pedals can make all the difference in your comfort and performance. Whether you choose the rugged Giro Rumble VR shoes or the minimalist Crankbrothers Eggbeater 3 pedals, make sure you invest in gear that's durable, comfortable, and versatile enough to handle any situation you might encounter on the road. Happy touring!
