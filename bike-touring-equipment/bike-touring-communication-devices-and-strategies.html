Bike Touring Communication Devices and Strategies

Bike touring is a great adventure that takes you to different places while enjoying the fresh air and beautiful views. However, staying connected with friends and family during your trip can be challenging. Fortunately, there are communication devices and strategies that can help you stay in touch no matter where you go.

Communication Devices

Mobile Phones

Mobile phones are the most common communication devices used by bike tourers. They are reliable and allow you to make calls, send text messages, and use data to access the internet. However, mobile phones should be used with caution as they can quickly drain their battery, leaving you without a means of communication.

Two-Way Radios

Two-way radios are another popular communication device for bike tourers as they operate on a radio frequency, allowing for longer ranges than a mobile phone. They are also useful in areas without mobile phone coverage. Two-way radios are lightweight, portable, and easy to use, making them a popular communication device for bike tourers.

Satellite Phones

Satellite phones are the most expensive communication devices for bike tourers, but they are also the most reliable. They operate using a satellite network, providing coverage in areas where mobile phone and two-way radio coverage is not available. Satellite phones can be rented or purchased, making them a popular choice for long-distance bike tours.

GPS Trackers

GPS trackers are not communication devices, but they can be used to share your location with friends and family. They are small and lightweight, making them ideal for bike tourers. GPS trackers provide real-time location updates, ensuring that your loved ones know where you are at all times.

Communication Strategies

Prepaid SIM Cards

Prepaid SIM cards are a convenient way of staying connected while on a bike tour. They are available in most countries and allow you to make calls and access the internet at a lower cost than using your home SIM card. Prepaid SIM cards are easy to purchase and activate, making them a popular communication strategy for bike tourers.

Social Media

Social media is a great way of sharing your bike tour with friends and family. You can post updates, photos, and videos to keep them informed of your progress. Social media platforms such as Facebook and Instagram also allow you to chat with friends and family, ensuring that you stay connected no matter the distance.

Email

Email is a popular communication strategy for bike tourers as it allows you to send messages and photos without the need for an internet connection. You can send and receive emails using a mobile phone or a laptop. Email is also useful for sending important information such as flight details or itinerary updates.

Conclusion

Communication devices and strategies are essential for bike tourers to stay connected with friends and family while on the road. Mobile phones, two-way radios, satellite phones, and GPS trackers are the most popular communication devices used by bike tourers. Prepaid SIM cards, social media, and email are effective communication strategies that can help you stay in touch no matter where you are.
